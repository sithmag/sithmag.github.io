## Hobbies

These are the interests that sit alongside my engineering life: routines and creative outlets that keep me curious, balanced, and constantly learning in different ways.

<h2 class="section-title">Vinyl &amp; Music</h2>
<p>
  I’ve recently started collecting vinyl records as a more intentional way to engage with music. I like the physicality of records: the cover art, the ritual, the presence they add to a room, and how they let me show a bit more of who I am through my space. I keep my record player ready so I can listen anytime, which makes the collection feel lived-in rather than purely decorative.
</p>

## Arts & Crafts
I've had a interest in this since Years 11 and 12 when I took Design and Technology as a subject and in high school when I took Metalworks and Woodworks consecutively as elective classes. I was involved in laser cutting projects, working with metal (annealing, casting, welding), Japanese wood joints, and 3D printing.
<br><br>
I've since gotten involved with more accessible forms of arts and crafts such as polymer clay and 3D origami. Although, I do still enjoy 3D printing and laser cutting given my access to them at my university. Sometimes this also comes in handy in engineering projects.

## Cooking & Baking
I've gotten into this art since I left home for university. I struggled with it initially but now I quite enjoy cooking and baking in my free time. It's a great way to unwind and also a very useful skill to have. With cooking I've found its very much a more forgiving task compared to baking which is more a science that I'm still learning more about with its interaction in certain ingredients. I've also gotten some interest in coffee brewing and latte art since buying a coffee machine.

## Photography
I've loved photography ever since I was a kid. Sometimes even editing certain pictures to bring out the beauty of them. Such as long exposure for writing with sparklers, and light manipulation. I used to be a lot more involved with this using photoshop and lightroom to edit my photos, but those apps are no longer provided by my uni, so I've stuck to raw photography. Since then, I've bought a film camera and digital camera to practice more and fit my aesthetic. I've found I really like the 2000s style of photography with a bit of grain and warm tones. I also really appreciate very high quality photos with great composition and lighting.

## Friends & Society
I've had many friends over my time in university. Some have been close friends, others more casual acquaintances. I've taken more initiative to meet more people and build friendships since starting uni. I've joined various clubs and societies to meet like-minded individuals and expand my social circle and cultural understanding. Some that have a love for food such as the Food Adventure Society, Multiracial Intercultural Experience Society (MIXSOC) for understanding and celebrating differences in others cultures, Sri Lankan Society (SLSOC) for learning more on my own heritage, and Sydney University Association of Biomedical Engineering (SUABE) for my professional life. In every single one these societies, 
<br><br>
I've also taken up leadership roles to help organise events and activities for members, further adding to my leadership skills. This was officially recognised by the university when I received an award from the Pro Vice Chancellor for Outstanding Service Award in 2021. I've also been involved as a residential assistant (RA) in my residential college (Regiment Building) to help with event planning and supporting new and existing students with their student life
